Mississippi LETRS® Training

Great news for Mississippi literacy educators! The Mississippi Department of Education, through the Office of Elementary Education and Reading, is offering LETRS® (Language Essentials for Teachers of Reading and Spelling) science of reading training. The LETRS course of study gives educators the skills they need to teach language and literacy to every student, even those with reading challenges like dyslexia.

LETRS is accredited by the IDA

Teacher Resources

LETRS is authored by literacy expert Dr. Louisa Moats and aligned to the Structured Literacy Approach based on the science of reading. Varied learning experiences include self-paced, online coursework, webinars, and face-to-face follow-up sessions. Flexible training options accommodate the challenges of our current times and educators’ schedules.

LETRS Helped Transform Mississippi

The Science of Reading Changes the Score

“LETRS training helped to integrate the science of reading. Teachers have a reason and a system as to how to implement research-based strategies to build better readers. The use of LETRS helped us to keep balanced literacy at the forefront of our reading instruction.”

—Shannon Whitehead, Principal

“Phoneme/Graphemes mapping - a wonderful way to help students discover how letters fit together to make words. Completely changed how I taught spelling.”

—Donna Walker, Teacher

“I have only been a principal for a short time at this school, but I previously served as a regional coordinator over the coach that was at this school. When MDE entered this building, teachers were not explicitly teaching phonics or utilizing literacy centers. Now the school has implemented Phonics First as well as having centers based on the five components of reading. There has been a drastic change in the way reading is taught.”

—Emily Eaton, Principal

“We keep student writing pieces in the hallway. The quality of our writing continues to improve as time passes. At the end of the second year, I was amazed at how far our writing had come. Our students were reading the text, understanding it, and then producing quality writing. To me, that was proof that what we were doing was working.”

—Brad Cox, Principal

“LETRS explains the why and then gives you the how. I know I am meeting my students needs while using the suggested activities.”

—Donna Walker, Teacher

“I would tell t[teachers] that this is the ultimate professional development and all teachers K-3rd grades, as well as principals should participate. It is research based and grounded in the science of reading. It makes an extraordinary difference in the reading instruction in schools.”

—Emily Eaton, Principal

“Our staff really bought into it. We took over a school that had been a D or F school for several years in a row. At the end of year two, we were a B rated school.”

—Brad Cox, Principal

“It was the best professional development I had as a teacher. As a literacy coach, I could see the impact it made on individual teachers and schools as a whole. The year the majority of my teachers were trained, the school moved from a D to a B rating. I also taught some college courses, Early Literacy I and II, at Blue Mountain College at night for teacher assistants. The first year it was taught with a traditional textbook. The next year, we used the LETRS books as our curriculum, and the difference was astounding. Teachers were better prepared for the classroom, more grounded in the science of reading, and they had a higher pass rate on the Foundations of Reading assessment. LETRS has been wonderful for our Mississippi teachers.”

—Angela Rutherford, Teacher

“My A-Ha moment was making a connection to implement LETRS fluidly from grades K-5. All of our teachers are "reading" teachers and phonics drills are implemented daily in grade K-5 to help close the reading level gaps that we faced at our school.”

—Shannon Whitehead, Principal

“[I would tell teachers to] go! We all need as many tools in our toolbox as possible. This training will benefit you and every student who enters your class.”

—Donna Walker, Teacher

“Our instruction shifted to focus more on phonics and phonemic awareness. We focused more on the basics to help improve our student achievement. Our staff really bought into our plan. —Brad Cox, Principal

“LETRS helped to build the knowledge base as to how children learn to read. In order to teach reading, we must understand the science of reading.
